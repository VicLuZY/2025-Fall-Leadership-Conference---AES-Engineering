FLC 2025 Main Session

1. Team Review of 5 Dysfunctions

Framework: Based on Patrick Lencioni's "Five Dysfunctions of a Team" model

Purpose: Assess team effectiveness and identify areas for improvement

The Five Dysfunctions Framework:
  • Absence of Trust: Team members don't feel comfortable being vulnerable
  • Fear of Conflict: Avoiding productive conflict leads to artificial harmony
  • Lack of Commitment: Without buy-in, team members don't commit to decisions
  • Avoidance of Accountability: Team members avoid calling out poor performance
  • Inattention to Results: Team members focus on individual goals over team success
  • Assessment: How are we performing on each of the 5 dysfunctions?
  • Cross-Company Unity: Evaluate synergy and collaboration across all offices
  • Recognition: Acknowledge leaders who excel at fostering collaboration and breaking down silos
  • Progress Review: What changes have been implemented since the last assessment?

2. Team Trust Building

Purpose: Strengthen interpersonal relationships and psychological safety within the team

Activities: Trust-building exercises, vulnerability-based leadership discussions, team bonding activities

Focus: Creating an environment where team members feel safe to take risks and be authentic

3. Rockefeller Habits Book Review

Framework: Based on Verne Harnish's "Scaling Up" methodology for business growth

Purpose: Develop a clear 1-year strategic plan and establish organizational themes

a. 3 Key Concepts: Priorities, Data & Rhythm
  • Priorities: Focus on the One Page Strategic Plan (OPSP) - a single-page document that captures the company's vision, mission, values, and key objectives
  • Data: Establish key performance indicators (KPIs) and ensure data is collected frequently enough to drive actionable insights and decision-making
  • Rhythm: Implement consistent meeting cadence - daily 15-minute huddles, weekly team meetings, monthly management reviews, quarterly planning sessions, and annual strategic planning
b. Key Categories (The 9 Essential Habits)
  1. Mastering the right people, doing the right things, right: Hiring, training, and aligning people with company values and objectives
  2. Mastering the one-page strategic plan: Creating and maintaining a clear, concise strategic document that guides all decisions
  3. Mastering the use of core values: Living and breathing company values in daily operations and decision-making
  4. Mastering organizational alignment and focus: Cascading strategy down through all levels of the organization
  5. Mastering the quarterly theme: Setting quarterly priorities that drive focused execution
  6. Mastering customer and employee feedback: Regular collection and action on feedback from key stakeholders
  7. Mastering the daily and weekly meetings: Structured, efficient meetings that drive accountability and progress
  8. Mastering the brand promise: Delivering consistent value that differentiates the company in the market
  9. Mastering cash flow: Understanding and optimizing cash flow for sustainable growth
c. OPSP (One Page Strategic Plan)

Purpose: Create a single-page strategic document that aligns the entire organization

  • Template Review: Examine OPSP structure including vision, mission, values, key metrics, and strategic priorities
  • Cascading Implementation: Create OPSP for AES corporate level, then adapt for each office location, then customize for each Practice Area (P&A)
  • Alignment Focus: Ensure all levels of the organization are aligned with corporate objectives
d. Deep Dive Session

Focus: "Mastering the right people, doing the right things, right"

  • Right People: Hiring criteria, cultural fit assessment, talent development programs
  • Right Things: Strategic priority setting, resource allocation, and focus areas
  • Right Way: Operational excellence, quality standards, and execution methodologies
